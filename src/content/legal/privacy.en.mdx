<Section id="games-data" num="§4" title="Games — data collected">

### Platform-managed data

Games distributed through Steam (Valve Corporation) and/or Epic Games Store (Epic Games Inc.) are subject to those platforms' own privacy policies. Each platform may collect account data, purchase history, playtime, and usage statistics independently. We encourage you to review:

- Valve Privacy Policy: [https://store.steampowered.com/privacy_agreement](https://store.steampowered.com/privacy_agreement)
- Epic Games Privacy Policy: [https://www.epicgames.com/site/en-US/privacypolicy](https://www.epicgames.com/site/en-US/privacypolicy)

### Multiplayer session data

When you participate in an online multiplayer session in *Velvet Door* (cooperative play for 2–4 players), the following technical data is processed to establish and maintain the session:

- **IP addresses** of connected players, processed transiently to route game traffic. IP addresses are not stored beyond the duration of the active session.
- **Platform identifiers** (Steam ID or equivalent), used to identify players within a session and enforce platform-level restrictions (bans, etc.).
- **Session metadata** (connection timestamps, latency measurements, session duration), retained for a maximum of **30 days** for the purpose of diagnosing connectivity issues.

No persistent player profiles, behavioural analytics, or advertising identifiers are maintained by Improbability Works from in-game data.

### Save data and game state

Game progress is stored locally on your device and, where Steam Cloud is enabled, synchronised to Valve's servers under their own privacy policy. Improbability Works does not have access to your local save data.

### Crash reports and diagnostics

Crash reporting at launch is handled exclusively through the platform SDKs integrated in the Game — specifically the **Steamworks SDK** (Valve Corporation) and the **Epic Online Services SDK** (Epic Games Inc.). Crash data collected through these integrations is processed by the respective platform under their own privacy policies, not by Improbability Works directly.

Improbability Works does not independently receive, store, or process crash dumps or diagnostic reports. Should we integrate a standalone crash-reporting tool in a future update, this policy will be updated before the feature ships and collection will be clearly disclosed in-game.

</Section>

<Section id="rights" num="§9" title="Your rights">

Under the GDPR and French law, you have the right to:

- **Access** — obtain confirmation of whether we process data about you, and receive a copy;
- **Rectification** — request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data;
- **Erasure** ("right to be forgotten") — request deletion of your data, where no legal ground overrides;
- **Restriction** — request that we limit processing in certain circumstances;
- **Portability** — receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format;
- **Objection** — object to processing based on legitimate interest;
- **Withdrawal of consent** — withdraw any consent given, at any time, without affecting the lawfulness of prior processing.

To exercise any of these rights, contact [privacy@improbabilityworks.studio](mailto:privacy@improbabilityworks.studio). We will respond within **30 days** (extendable to 3 months for complex requests, with prior notice).

If you believe your rights have not been respected, you may lodge a complaint with the **CNIL** (Commission Nationale de l'Informatique et des Libertés): [https://www.cnil.fr](https://www.cnil.fr).

</Section>
